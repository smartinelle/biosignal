# Venture Positioning Notes

## Pattern from comparable companies

Recent AI-health companies are not positioning as generic assistants. They attach agents to concrete workflows with obvious buyers, outputs, and ROI.

- **Lattice / Wedge-like healthcare agent infrastructure:** custom workflow automation, legacy connectors, human-in-loop, audit trails, governance, monitoring.
- **Uncovr:** surgical video → operative reports + CPT coding + clinical intelligence; clear hospital/revenue-cycle buyer and concrete output.
- **Hedera Dx lab-solutions pattern:** lab-to-report workflow, tumor profiling panels, software analysis, validation/support. The value is not "AI understands cancer"; it is an integrated lab workflow from sample/data to actionable report.

## Implication for BioSignal Navigator

Do not pitch as a broad research assistant.

Pitch as:

> An agent-human workflow for biotech R&D teams to troubleshoot ambiguous experiments and choose the next best measurement.

## Wedge

Initial wedge: **living tissue systems**.

Why this wedge works:

- visually original for a hackathon
- close to Sacha's thesis on ex-vivo biological state
- broad enough to include organoids, organ-on-chip, tissue engineering QC, perfusion, preservation, and preclinical tissue experiments
- less clinically regulated than patient-care decision support
- produces a concrete output: troubleshooting memo + next measurements

## Buyer/user hypothesis

Likely users:

- tissue engineering startups
- organoid / organ-on-chip companies
- preclinical R&D teams
- translational biology groups
- regenerative medicine teams
- lab automation / assay development teams

Core pain:

> Ambiguous biological readouts consume senior-scientist time and waste experiment cycles because the team must manually connect observations, mechanisms, literature, protocols, and assay options.

## Product output

A structured troubleshooting memo:

1. observed signals
2. plausible failure mechanisms
3. evidence and caveats
4. next best measurements
5. uncertainty bottleneck
6. human expert review question

## Hackathon-safe claim

> The architecture is general for biotech experiment troubleshooting; today's demo vertical is living tissue state interpretation.

## Claims to avoid

- predicts viability
- diagnoses disease
- recommends treatment
- decides transplant/discard
- infers molecular truth from macro variables

## Winning artifact thesis

BioSignal Navigator should win by showing that the strongest AI-agent products in health/biotech are not generic chatbots. They are workflow systems that know their data source, output, buyer, handoff, and uncertainty boundary.
